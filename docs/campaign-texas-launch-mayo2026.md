# Campaign brief — *Lanzamiento Texas: HazloAsíYa en Houston y San Antonio*

> Sprint de 2 semanas, 16–29 mayo 2026. Awareness + partner pipeline + revenue inicial. Estrategia en inglés; copy al público en español, listo para enviar.

---

## 1. Campaign overview

**Name**: *Lanzamiento Texas — HazloAsíYa llega a Houston y San Antonio* (corto: *Llegamos a Texas*)

**One-line summary**: A 2-week regional launch in greater Houston and greater San Antonio, using free seasonal guides (school enrollment, ayuda luz/gas) as the awareness magnet, **Kit SNAP** as the paid conversion driver, and concurrent outreach to 10–15 nonprofits/consulates to open the `/para-organizaciones/` partner pipeline.

**Window**: 2026-05-16 → 2026-05-29 (14 days). Soft launch on day 1 (organic + email); paid creative live by day 3.

### Primary objective — Awareness in TX Hispanic households
**Reach 75,000–150,000 unique Hispanic adults** in Houston DMA + San Antonio DMA across paid + organic + earned by 2026-05-29, with **1,000–2,500 net-new site sessions** from those markets (currently near-zero baseline).

### Secondary objectives
| | Target |
|---|---|
| Email signups (Mailchimp, segmented by TX state) | 200–500 |
| **Partner conversations opened** with TX nonprofits / consulados | 8–12 |
| Partner letters-of-interest or pilot meetings booked | 2–4 |
| Kit SNAP purchases (modest — 2-week window) | 15–30 |
| Press/community mentions in TX Spanish media | 1–3 |

> Revenue is **not** the primary KPI in this sprint. Awareness + partner pipeline + email list growth are the load-bearing metrics. Revenue is a leading indicator we'll watch but won't optimize against.

## 2. Target audience

### Geographic concentration
- **Houston DMA**: Harris, Fort Bend, Montgomery, Brazoria counties. ~45% Hispanic population in city core, ~38% metro. Mexican-American majority; growing Honduran and Salvadoran communities.
- **San Antonio DMA**: Bexar, Comal, Guadalupe counties. ~64% Hispanic. Heavily Mexican-American, deep multi-generational roots.

### Primary segment — "Mamás puente" (TX edition)
Hispanic mothers, 25–45, household income at or near Texas SNAP eligibility (~$32k for 3, $40k for 4), with school-age kids. Spanish-dominant or bilingual. Heavy WhatsApp users. Trust circle: comadres, parroquia, escuela, HEB cashier (yes, really — community node).

**TX-specific pain points**:
- Lone Star Card / Your Texas Benefits portal is hard to use in Spanish; H1010 form is famously confusing.
- Texas summer heat (95°F+ from late May) sends AC bills from $80 → $250+; LIHEAP and city assistance are real but opaque.
- School enrollment opens in May; HISD and SAISD documentation requirements catch many families off-guard (especially birth certs apostille, proof-of-residence).
- Stricter-than-average TX eligibility rules on adult Medicaid → families think they don't qualify for *anything*, miss CHIP for their kids.
- Mixed-status households are common — fear of "carga pública" is louder here than in CA/NY.

### Secondary segment — "Recién llegados"
Families in TX < 5 years, often connected via consulados or church networks. Reached through partners, not paid.

### Where they spend time (TX-specific)
- **WhatsApp** — the dominant channel. Forwarded screenshots and PDFs travel further than ads.
- **Facebook**: groups *"Madres hispanas en Houston"*, *"Hispanos en San Antonio"*, *"HEB shoppers"*, *"Houston-Pasadena en español"*, neighborhood marketplace groups.
- **Instagram + TikTok**: younger half of segment; *#HoustonHispano*, *#SanAntonio*, *#TejanosEnEspañol*.
- **YouTube**: searches for "como llenar H1010", "documentos para escuela Texas".
- **Univision Houston (KXLN-45)** + **Telemundo Houston (KTMD-47)** + **Univision San Antonio (KWEX-41)** + their digital properties — high trust, surprisingly affordable digital ad placements ($300–800/wk).
- **HEB shopper communities** (in-store flyer boards in mostly-Hispanic stores; community engagement via HEB's *"Helping Here"* foundation programs).
- **Parroquias**: Houston (Catedral del Sagrado Corazón, Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe), San Antonio (San Fernando Cathedral, Mission San José).
- **Consulado de México en Houston** y **en San Antonio** — both run Ventanilla de Asistencia Jurídica + Ventanilla de Salud programs. Hispanic-services partner gold.

## 3. Key messages

### Core message
**"Llegamos a Texas. La guía en español que tu familia necesita — sin filas, sin enredos."**

### Supporting messages by content angle

| Angle | Message | Use in |
|---|---|---|
| **Heat / utility relief** | *"Antes que llegue el calor de junio: aprende cómo pedir ayuda para pagar la luz."* | Ayuda luz/gas guide + Meta carousel |
| **School enrollment** | *"Inscribir a tus hijos en la escuela en Houston o San Antonio: la lista exacta de documentos."* | School-enrollment blog + TikTok demo |
| **SNAP** | *"Cuando se acaben las comidas escolares en junio, no esperes. En 3 minutos sabes si tu familia califica."* | SNAP funnel + email + Meta lead Reel |

| **Anti-objection** | *"Aplicar a SNAP, Medicaid de niños o LIHEAP no es carga pública para la mayoría de las familias."* | Email, FAQ, blog, ad copy |

### Message hierarchy for the launch
1. **Why care?** — Texas summer is hard. Bills suben, comidas escolares se acaban, hay que inscribir a los niños.
2. **What's the solution?** — Una sola guía, en español, gratis para arrancar.
3. **Why HazloAsíYa?** — En español de verdad, sin compartir tus datos con el gobierno, sin abogados caros.
4. **What to do?** — Empieza con la guía gratis que más te sirva hoy.

## 9. Risks and mitigations

### Risk 1 — Timeline (HIGH)
**2 days to launch.** Same risk as before; even tighter at sprint scale.

**Mitigation**: D1 launch = email broadcast + organic only. Ad pack live by D3. Communicate "lanzamiento oficial: lunes 19 de mayo" if needed to keep promises honest.

### Risk 2 — Partner pipeline won't close in 2 weeks (HIGH)
Nonprofits and consulados operate on 4–12 week procurement / approval cycles. Two weeks **opens** conversations; it does not close partnerships.

**Mitigation**: Reframe internally: the KPI is **conversations opened + meetings booked**, not signed partnerships. Set expectation that signed partner agreements arrive in Fase 2 (June–July). The 2-week sprint creates the pipeline; the next 4–6 weeks convert it.

### Risk 3 — "Carga pública" + general government distrust (MEDIUM-HIGH in TX)
Heightened in TX vs. CA/NY because of state political climate and proximity to immigration enforcement actions.

**Mitigation**:
- Every paid creative includes anti-carga-pública message somewhere.
- All landing pages link the privacy policy prominently.
- Lead with the *free* guides (school enrollment, luz/gas) before SNAP — lower trust threshold to start the relationship.
- Avoid imagery / language that could read as government-affiliated.

### Risk 4 — TX-only ad delivery underdelivers (MEDIUM)
Concentrated geo can mean Meta runs out of fresh audience after 5–7 days, CPMs rise, performance drops.

**Mitigation**:
- Plan for creative refresh on D6–7 and D11.
- Have an audience-expansion fallback ready: if delivery stalls, expand to Dallas + Austin Hispanic audiences with the same creative (still TX, still on-strategy, more inventory).

### Risk 5 — Compliance (LOW but serious)
The "no asesoría legal ni gubernamental" disclaimer must appear in all paid creative metadata and on the partner one-pager. TX has aggressive state-level UPL (unauthorized practice of law) enforcement — anything that reads as legal advice is real exposure.

**Mitigation**: Every piece reviewed against `lib/legal-texts.ts`. The existing `npm run audit:legal` script catches most issues — run it before sprint launch.
